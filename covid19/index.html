Did COVID‑19 appear sooner than January?

Was it confused with flu data? Have we already flattened the curve?

Updated April 26, 2020.

Update 2020-04-26: Graph data updated for week 17 actual deaths. Separated IHME and added covid19-projections.com (MIT) projections. IHME estimates are up a little to 68K deaths (mean) by Aug 4. COVID‑19 Projections are 156K (mean) by Aug 4. New information on "when did COVID‑19 appear" is available from the Nexstrain team showing only a handful of cases in January that match the COVID‑19 genome.

Update 2020-04-19: Graph data updated for week 16 actual deaths. Overall estimates remain around 60K deaths. Separated 2019-2020 ILI data from COVID‑19 in graph.

Update 2020-04-12: Graph data updated for week 15 actual and estimated deaths. Overall estimates have lowered to 60K deaths (from 100-250K) through August.

Update 2020-04-08: CDC COVIDView Week 13, ending March 28, 2020 (PDF) describes seeing the COVID‑19 symptoms back in November-December, but doesn't explain the increase in cases starting week 13 (it actually started earlier, but not where it would be from Nov-Dec).


There are theories (1, 2) going around that COVID‑19 has been around since November-December (marked "A" on the chart) and the current response is an over-reaction to a normal, although severe, Influenza-like illness (ILI).

The graph below shows our most recent severe flu season in 2017-2018 in blue, where we peaked around 5500 deaths per week for two weeks (CDC) and had a yearly estimated deaths of 61,000 (based on CDC data).

It also shows our current flu season in red, with CDC flu data up through week 12 (Mar 22), combined and followed by COVID‑19 deaths (Wikipedia) through week 16, and estimated weekly deaths through week 19 (IHME). Social-distancing recommendations announced March 13 (marked "C").

I think it's clearly visible that COVID‑19 did not start before January and we did not "already flatten the curve" before starting social distancing.
