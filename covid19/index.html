Did COVID-19 appear sooner than January?

Was it confused with flu data? Have we already flattened the curve?

Graph data last updated April 6, 2020.

Text last updated April 8, 2020.

Update 2020-04-08: COVIDView Week 13, ending March 28, 2020 (PDF) describes seeing the COVID-19 symptoms back in November-December, but doesn't explain the increase in cases starting week 13 (it actually started earlier, this is just when the exponentional curve really kicked-in).


There are theories (1, 2) going around that COVID-19 has been around since November-December (marked "A" on the chart) and the current response is an over-reaction to a normal, although severe, Influenza-like illness (ILI).

The graph below shows our most recent severe flu season in 2017-2018 in blue, where we peaked around 5500 deaths per week for two weeks (CDC) and had a yearly estimated deaths of 61,000 (based on CDC data).

It also shows our current flu season in red, with CDC flu data up through week 12 (Mar 22), combined and followed by COVID-19 deaths (Wikipedia) through week 14, and estimated weekly deaths through week 18 (IHME). Note the strong downward curve on the flu after social-distancing (marked "C") and before the COVID-19 picked up (and assuming all pneumonia and influenza death counts are currently counted as COVID-19 deaths).

I think it's clearly visible that COVID-19 did not start before January and we did not "already flatten the curve" before starting social distancing.
